1.6.6) The two random numbers are NOT actually truly random in mathematical 
sense because these random numbers (actually pseudo-random) are generated 
from an initial seed. Because of this seed, the order in which you will see 
random numbers is the same. The TSA recently paid big money to a man who 
created a supposedly truly random generator for assigning which lines a 
passenger must go to for security check-ins.
1.6.7) The probability that a number will be shown correctly is much less 
than 1%. The way I got this value is by attempting to check overflow using 
first a simple 2 bit, then a 3 bit, and finally a 4 bit multiplication table. 
I observed that every time I increased the bit amount, the number of 
non-overflows decreased by roughly 20%. Thus extrapolating for a large, 32 or
64 bit value, the probability that the product is mathematically correct (it 
did not overflow) will be miniscule.
